Search engine optimisation in New Zealand has changed dramatically. AI Overviews now appear on over 30% of commercial searches. Zero-click results are rising. And the old playbook of stuffing keywords into blog posts no longer works.

But organic search is still the highest-ROI channel for most businesses. The companies that adapt to how search works today will win the next decade of traffic.

## What has changed in 2026

Google’s AI Overviews now summarise answers directly in search results. For informational queries, this means fewer clicks to websites. But for commercial and transactional queries, searchers still click through because they need to compare, purchase or contact a business.

This is why keyword intent matters more than ever. Targeting high-volume informational keywords is less valuable if Google answers them directly. Targeting commercial-intent keywords like “SEO agency Auckland” or “SEO services NZ” still drives qualified traffic because searchers want to evaluate and engage with a provider.

## What still works

**Technical foundations:** Site speed, mobile responsiveness, clean architecture, proper schema markup and crawlability remain non-negotiable.

**Content depth:** Thin pages get filtered out. Pages that demonstrate genuine expertise, cover a topic comprehensively and provide actionable value continue to rank.

**Authority signals:** Backlinks from relevant, authoritative NZ sites still move the needle. Digital PR, expert commentary and strategic partnerships are the most effective link-building approaches.

## The AEO opportunity

AI Engine Optimisation is the practice of structuring your content so AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google Gemini) cite and recommend your business. This means clear entity markup, structured data, FAQ sections and content written in a way that AI models can extract and attribute.

Businesses that invest in AEO now will have a significant advantage as AI-driven discovery becomes mainstream.
